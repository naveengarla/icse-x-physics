## Topics Covered

### 6.1 Dispersion of White Light
- **Dispersion**: splitting of white light into its constituent colours when it passes through a prism
- Cause: different colours have different refractive indices in glass; violet is deviated most, red least
- **Spectrum produced**: VIBGYOR — Violet, Indigo, Blue, Green, Yellow, Orange, Red
- **Recombination**: using a second inverted prism, the spectrum recombines into white light

### 6.2 The Prism and Deviation
- A glass prism refracts light twice (entering and leaving the prism)
- **Angle of deviation**: angle between incident ray and emergent ray
- Minimum deviation when the prism is in a symmetrical position
- Deviation ∝ refractive index of colour (violet deviated most, red least)

### 6.3 Electromagnetic Spectrum
Visible light is a tiny part of the electromagnetic spectrum. All EM waves travel at `c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s` in vacuum.

| Radiation | Wavelength Range | Sources / Uses |
|---|---|---|
| Radio Waves | > 1 mm | Radio, TV, mobile communication |
| Microwaves | 1 mm – 1 m | Radar, microwave ovens, satellite communication |
| Infrared (IR) | 700 nm – 1 mm | Remote controls, thermal imaging, night-vision, heating |
| **Visible Light** | **400 nm – 700 nm** | **Vision** |
| Ultraviolet (UV) | 10 nm – 400 nm | Sterilisation, fluorescent lamps, detecting forgeries |
| X-Rays | 0.01 nm – 10 nm | Medical imaging, detecting cracks, airport security |
| Gamma (γ) Rays | < 0.01 nm | Cancer treatment, sterilising food, nuclear reactions |

### 6.4 Colours of White Light
- White light = mixture of all visible colours
- Primary colours of light: **Red, Green, Blue** (additive mixing)
- Secondary colours: Cyan, Magenta, Yellow
- Complementary colours: pairs that combine to give white (e.g., Red + Cyan)
- Colour of an object = colour of light it **reflects** (absorbs all others)
  - Red object in green light → appears **black** (no red light to reflect)

### 6.5 Scattering of Light — Tyndall Effect
- **Tyndall Effect**: scattering of light by tiny particles suspended in a medium (colloid)
  - Beam of light becomes visible (e.g., sunbeam in dusty room, headlights in fog)
- **Rayleigh Scattering**: scattering by molecules; intensity ∝ 1/λ⁴ (shorter wavelengths scatter more)
  - **Blue sky**: blue light (short wavelength) is scattered more by air molecules
  - **Red sunrise/sunset**: at low angles, light travels through more atmosphere → blue scattered away → red/orange remains
  - **White clouds**: water droplets are large; all wavelengths scatter equally → white
  - **Red danger signals**: red light has longest wavelength → scattered least → travels furthest

---

## Key Points

| Phenomenon | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Sky is blue | Blue light scattered most (Rayleigh scattering, λ short) |
| Sunset/sunrise is red | Blue scattered away; red travels through long path |
| Clouds are white | Large water droplets scatter all wavelengths equally |
| Deep sea water appears blue | Blue light penetrates deepest |

---

## Important Terms

- **Dispersion**: Splitting of white light into its component colours by a prism
- **VIBGYOR**: Order of colours in the visible spectrum (Violet to Red)
- **Electromagnetic Spectrum**: Complete range of all electromagnetic radiations
- **Tyndall Effect**: Scattering of light by colloidal particles
- **Rayleigh Scattering**: Scattering of light by molecules; responsible for blue sky
