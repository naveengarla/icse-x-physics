## Topics Covered

### 2.1 Work
- **Work** is done when a force causes displacement in the direction of the force
- Formula: `W = F × d × cos θ`
  - θ = angle between force and displacement
  - If force and displacement are in same direction: `W = F × d`
- SI unit: Joule (J) = Newton × metre
- Work is a **scalar** quantity
- **Zero work**: when θ = 90° (e.g., carrying a bag horizontally, moon orbiting Earth)
- **Negative work**: when θ > 90° (e.g., friction opposing motion)

### 2.2 Energy
- **Energy**: Capacity of a body to do work
- SI unit: Joule (J)
- Energy is a **scalar** quantity

#### Kinetic Energy (KE)
- Energy possessed by a body due to its **motion**
- Formula: `KE = ½mv²`
- Factors: mass and velocity (velocity has more impact — squared)

#### Potential Energy (PE)
- Energy possessed by a body due to its **position or configuration**
- **Gravitational PE**: `PE = mgh`
- **Elastic PE**: energy stored in a compressed/stretched spring (e.g., bow, spring balance)

#### Principle of Conservation of Energy
- Energy can neither be created nor destroyed; it can only be transformed from one form to another
- Total mechanical energy (KE + PE) remains constant in the absence of friction
- Example — freely falling body:
  - At top: PE = max, KE = 0
  - At bottom: KE = max, PE = 0
  - At any point: PE + KE = constant

### 2.3 Power
- **Power**: Rate of doing work (or rate of energy transfer)
- Formula: `P = W/t = F × v`
- SI unit: Watt (W) = Joule per second
- 1 horsepower (hp) = 746 W

### 2.4 Commercial Unit of Energy
- **Kilowatt-hour (kWh)**: commercial unit used by electricity boards
- `1 kWh = 1000 W × 3600 s = 3.6 × 10⁶ J`
- Electricity bill calculation: Units consumed = Power (kW) × Time (h)

### 2.5 Different Forms of Energy
- Mechanical (kinetic + potential), thermal, chemical, electrical, nuclear, light, sound
- **Renewable sources**: solar, wind, hydro, tidal, geothermal
- **Non-renewable sources**: coal, oil, natural gas, nuclear fuel

---

## Important Terms

- **Work**: Product of force and displacement in the direction of force
- **Energy**: Ability to do work
- **Kinetic Energy**: Energy due to motion
- **Potential Energy**: Energy due to position or configuration
- **Power**: Rate of doing work
- **Conservation of Energy**: Total energy of an isolated system remains constant

---

## Common Misconceptions
- Holding a heavy load stationary does NO work (no displacement)
- A body can have KE and PE simultaneously (e.g., a ball thrown upward at mid-height)
