## Chapter Summaries

### [Chapter 1 — Force](./01-Force/README.md)
- Turning forces; moment of a force; principle of moments
- Centre of gravity; stable, unstable and neutral equilibrium
- Uniform circular motion; centripetal force

### [Chapter 2 — Work, Energy and Power](./02-Work-Energy-and-Power/README.md)
- Work done by a force; zero and negative work
- Kinetic energy (`KE = ½mv²`) and gravitational potential energy (`PE = mgh`)
- Conservation of mechanical energy; power; commercial unit of energy (kWh)

### [Chapter 3 — Machines](./03-Machines/README.md)
- Mechanical advantage, velocity ratio, efficiency
- Classes of levers (1st, 2nd, 3rd) with examples
- Pulley systems: single fixed, single movable, block and tackle

### [Chapter 4 — Refraction of Light at Plane Surfaces](./04-Refraction-of-Light-at-Plane-Surfaces/README.md)
- Laws of refraction; Snell's Law; refractive index
- Refraction through a glass slab; lateral displacement
- Total internal reflection; critical angle; optical fibres; prisms; mirage

### [Chapter 5 — Refraction Through a Lens](./05-Refraction-Through-a-Lens/README.md)
- Convex and concave lenses; image formation (all positions)
- Lens formula (`1/v − 1/u = 1/f`); magnification; power of a lens
- Defects of vision (myopia, hypermetropia, presbyopia) and their corrections

### [Chapter 6 — Spectrum](./06-Spectrum/README.md)
- Dispersion of white light through a prism; VIBGYOR
- Electromagnetic spectrum (radio to gamma rays)
- Colours; Tyndall effect; blue sky; red sunrise/sunset

### [Chapter 7 — Sound](./07-Sound/README.md)
- Reflection of sound; echo; SONAR; reverberation
- Natural, damped and forced vibrations; resonance
- Loudness, pitch, quality; ultrasound and infrasound; noise pollution

### [Chapter 8 — Current Electricity](./08-Current-Electricity/README.md)
- Ohm's Law; resistance; resistivity; series and parallel circuits
- EMF and internal resistance; terminal voltage
- Electrical power, energy and heating effect (Joule's law)

### [Chapter 9 — Household Circuits](./09-Household-Circuits/README.md)
- Live, neutral and earth wires; domestic wiring
- Safety devices: fuse, MCB, ELCB; earthing
- Short circuit and overloading; electrical safety precautions

### [Chapter 10 — Electro-magnetism](./10-Electromagnetism/README.md)
- Oersted's experiment; magnetic field around conductors and solenoids
- Electromagnets; electric bell; force on current-carrying conductor; Fleming's LHR
- Electric motor; electromagnetic induction; AC/DC generators; transformer

### [Chapter 11 — Calorimetry](./11-Calorimetry/README.md)
- Specific heat capacity (`Q = mcΔT`); principle of calorimetry
- Latent heat of fusion and vaporisation; heating/cooling curves
- Refrigeration (vapour compression); greenhouse effect and global warming

### [Chapter 12 — Radioactivity](./12-Radioactivity/README.md)
- Types of radiation (α, β, γ) — properties compared
- Half-life; radioactive decay equation
- Uses in medicine and industry; safety precautions
- Nuclear fission; chain reaction; nuclear reactor; nuclear fusion; X-rays

## Quick Reference — Key Formulae

| Formula | Description |
|---|---|
| `τ = F × d` | Moment of force |
| `F = mv²/r` | Centripetal force |
| `W = Fd cosθ` | Work done |
| `KE = ½mv²` | Kinetic energy |
| `PE = mgh` | Gravitational potential energy |
| `P = W/t` | Power |
| `MA = L/E` | Mechanical advantage |
| `η = (MA/VR) × 100%` | Efficiency of machine |
| `n₁ sin i = n₂ sin r` | Snell's Law (refraction) |
| `sin C = 1/n` | Critical angle |
| `1/v − 1/u = 1/f` | Lens formula |
| `P = 1/f (m)` | Power of a lens (Dioptre) |
| `v = fλ` | Wave speed |
| `V = IR` | Ohm's Law |
| `R = ρL/A` | Resistance and resistivity |
| `P = VI = I²R = V²/R` | Electrical power |
| `Vs/Vp = Ns/Np` | Transformer equation |
| `Q = mcΔT` | Sensible heat formula |
| `Q = mL` | Latent heat formula |
| `N = N₀ × (½)ⁿ` | Radioactive decay (half-lives) |
| `E = mc²` | Mass–energy equivalence |
