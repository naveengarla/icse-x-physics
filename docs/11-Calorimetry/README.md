## Topics Covered

### 11.1 Heat and Temperature
- **Heat**: form of energy that flows from a hotter body to a colder body; SI unit: Joule (J)
- **Temperature**: degree of hotness or coldness; SI unit: Kelvin (K)
- Relationship: `T(K) = T(°C) + 273`
- Heat and temperature are different: large body at low temperature may have more heat energy than a small body at high temperature

### 11.2 Specific Heat Capacity
- **Specific heat capacity (c)**: heat required to raise the temperature of **1 kg** of a substance by **1 K (or 1°C)**
- Formula: `Q = mcΔT`
  - Q = heat absorbed/released (J)
  - m = mass (kg)
  - c = specific heat capacity (J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹)
  - ΔT = change in temperature (°C or K)
- Water has a very high specific heat capacity: **4200 J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹**
  - This makes water an excellent coolant and moderates coastal climates

| Substance | Specific Heat (J/kg/K) |
|---|---|
| Water | 4200 |
| Ice | 2100 |
| Steam | 2100 |
| Aluminium | 900 |
| Copper | 385 |
| Iron | 460 |
| Glass | 670 |
| Mercury | 140 |

### 11.3 Principle of Calorimetry
- When a hot and cold body are mixed in an insulated container (calorimeter):  
  **Heat lost by hot body = Heat gained by cold body**
- Assumes no heat exchange with surroundings
- `m₁c₁(T₁ − Tf) = m₂c₂(Tf − T₂)`
  - T₁ = initial temp of hot body, T₂ = initial temp of cold body, Tf = final mixture temperature

### 11.4 Change of State
- Melting, boiling, condensation, solidification — phase transitions at constant temperature

#### Latent Heat
- **Latent heat**: heat absorbed or released during a change of state at **constant temperature**
- Formula: `Q = mL`
  - L = specific latent heat (J/kg)

| Process | Latent Heat | Value for Water |
|---|---|---|
| Melting of ice | Specific Latent Heat of Fusion (Lf) | 336,000 J/kg (336 kJ/kg) |
| Boiling of water | Specific Latent Heat of Vaporisation (Lv) | 2,260,000 J/kg (2260 kJ/kg) |

- Why burns from steam are more severe than from boiling water: steam releases extra 2260 kJ/kg as it condenses
- Why ice is used to cool drinks: ice absorbs 336 kJ/kg as it melts (much more than same mass of cold water)

### 11.5 Heating and Cooling Curves
- **Heating curve** of water from ice to steam:
  - Sloped section: temperature rising, solid/liquid/gas heating up (`Q = mcΔT`)
  - Flat section (plateau): phase change at constant temperature (`Q = mL`)
  - Two plateaus: at 0°C (melting) and 100°C (boiling)
- Similar shape for cooling curve (in reverse)

### 11.6 Anomalous Expansion of Water
- Water is most dense at **4°C**
- Below 4°C, water expands on cooling (anomalous behaviour)
- Consequence: ice forms at surface; water at 4°C sinks → aquatic life survives in winter

### 11.7 Refrigeration (Vapour Compression Cycle)
- **Working principle**: evaporation of refrigerant absorbs heat from inside the fridge → cooling
- **Cycle**: compressor → condenser → expansion valve → evaporator → back to compressor
- Refrigerant evaporates in evaporator (inside fridge) → absorbs heat
- Refrigerant condenses in condenser (outside/back of fridge) → releases heat
- Common refrigerants: Freon (R-22, R-134a); CFCs being phased out (harmful to ozone layer)

### 11.8 Greenhouse Effect and Global Warming
- **Greenhouse effect**:
  - Short-wave solar radiation passes through atmosphere and heats Earth's surface
  - Earth emits long-wave infrared radiation
  - Greenhouse gases (CO₂, methane, water vapour, CFCs) absorb this and re-emit it back → warming
  - Natural greenhouse effect is essential for life
- **Enhanced greenhouse effect**: excess CO₂ and other GHGs trap more heat → global warming
- **Effects of global warming**: melting ice caps, rising sea levels, extreme weather events, habitat loss
- **Mitigation**: renewable energy, energy efficiency, reducing deforestation, international agreements (Paris Agreement)

## Important Terms

- **Calorimetry**: Measurement and study of heat exchange
- **Specific Heat Capacity**: Heat per unit mass per unit temperature change
- **Latent Heat**: Heat absorbed/released during change of state at constant temperature
- **Greenhouse Effect**: Trapping of Earth's heat radiation by atmospheric gases
- **Global Warming**: Rise in average Earth temperature due to enhanced greenhouse effect
