## Topics Covered

### 8.1 Electric Current and Potential Difference
- **Electric current (I)**: rate of flow of charge; `I = Q/t`; SI unit: Ampere (A)
- **Potential difference (V)**: work done per unit charge; `V = W/Q`; SI unit: Volt (V)
- Conventional current flows from + to −; electron flow is from − to +

### 8.2 Ohm's Law
- **Ohm's Law**: At constant temperature, current through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference across it  
  `V = IR`  
  `R = V/I`
- **Resistance (R)**: opposition offered to flow of current; SI unit: Ohm (Ω)
- Ohmic conductors: follow Ohm's law (metallic conductors at constant temperature)
- Non-ohmic conductors: diode, filament lamp, thermistor

### 8.3 Resistance — Factors and Resistivity
- **Factors affecting resistance**:
  - Length (L): R ∝ L
  - Cross-sectional area (A): R ∝ 1/A
  - Material (resistivity ρ)
  - Temperature (resistance increases with temperature for metals)
- **Resistivity formula**: `R = ρL/A`; SI unit of ρ: Ω·m
- Good conductors: low resistivity (copper, silver)
- Insulators: high resistivity (rubber, glass)

### 8.4 Series and Parallel Circuits

#### Series Circuit
- Same current flows through all components
- Total resistance: `R = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + …`
- Voltage divides: `V = V₁ + V₂ + V₃`
- Total resistance is always greater than any individual resistance

#### Parallel Circuit
- Same potential difference across all components
- Current divides: `I = I₁ + I₂ + I₃`
- Total resistance: `1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃`
- Total resistance is always less than the smallest individual resistance

### 8.5 EMF and Internal Resistance
- **EMF (ε)**: work done by the source per unit charge; total energy provided
- **Internal resistance (r)**: resistance offered by the electrolyte/electrodes inside the cell
- **Terminal voltage**: `V = ε − Ir` (during discharge)
- When no current flows: terminal voltage = EMF
- `ε = V + Ir` → `V = ε − Ir`

### 8.6 Electrical Power and Energy
- **Electrical Power**: `P = VI = I²R = V²/R`; SI unit: Watt (W)
- **Electrical Energy**: `E = Pt = VIt = I²Rt`; SI unit: Joule (J)
- **Commercial unit**: kilowatt-hour (kWh)
  - `1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J`
  - Cost of electricity = Units (kWh) × Rate per unit
- **Heating effect** (Joule's law): `H = I²Rt`
  - Applications: electric heater, iron, geyser, electric bulb

### 8.7 Combination Problems
- Mixed circuits (series-parallel combinations)
- Always simplify by finding equivalent resistance step by step
- Use `V = IR` for each section

## Important Terms

- **Ohm's Law**: V ∝ I at constant temperature and other physical conditions
- **Resistance**: Opposition to current flow; depends on material, length, area, temperature
- **Resistivity**: Intrinsic resistance property of a material; independent of shape/size
- **EMF**: Total electrical energy supplied per unit charge by a source
- **Terminal Voltage**: Actual potential difference at the terminals when current flows

## Comparison: Series vs Parallel

| Feature | Series | Parallel |
|---|---|---|
| Current | Same throughout | Divides |
| Voltage | Divides | Same across all |
| Total Resistance | Sum of all | Less than smallest |
| If one component fails | Entire circuit breaks | Others continue working |
| Use | Not preferred for household | Preferred for household |
