# Chapter 5: Refraction Through a Lens

> **Selina Concise Physics — Class X ICSE**
> **Part of Unit: Light | Semester 1**

---

## Topics Covered

### 5.1 Types of Lenses
- **Convex lens** (converging): thicker at centre; converges parallel rays at principal focus
- **Concave lens** (diverging): thinner at centre; diverges parallel rays; appears to come from principal focus

### 5.2 Key Terminology
- **Optical centre (O)**: central point of the lens through which light passes undeviated
- **Principal axis**: line passing through optical centre perpendicular to lens plane
- **Principal focus (F)**: point where parallel rays converge (convex) or appear to diverge from (concave) after refraction
- **Focal length (f)**: distance between optical centre and principal focus
- **Radius of curvature (R)**: `R = 2f` (approx. for thin lenses)
- Two focal points: F₁ (first focus) and F₂ (second focus) on opposite sides

### 5.3 Image Formation by Lenses (Ray Diagrams)
Use 2 of these 3 standard rays:
1. Ray parallel to principal axis → passes through F₂ (convex) / appears to come from F₂ (concave)
2. Ray through optical centre → passes undeviated
3. Ray through F₁ → emerges parallel to principal axis (convex)

#### Convex Lens — Image Positions

| Object Position | Image Position | Nature of Image |
|---|---|---|
| At infinity | At F₂ | Real, inverted, highly diminished |
| Beyond 2F₁ | Between F₂ and 2F₂ | Real, inverted, diminished |
| At 2F₁ | At 2F₂ | Real, inverted, same size |
| Between F₁ and 2F₁ | Beyond 2F₂ | Real, inverted, magnified |
| At F₁ | At infinity | Real, inverted, infinitely large |
| Between O and F₁ | Same side as object | Virtual, erect, magnified |

#### Concave Lens — Image Position
- Always forms a **virtual, erect, diminished** image on the same side as the object
- Image lies between O and F₁ for any object position

### 5.4 Sign Convention for Lenses (New Cartesian)
- Distances measured from optical centre
- Along incident light → positive; against incident light → negative
- Object always on left → `u` is negative
- Focal length of convex lens → positive (+f)
- Focal length of concave lens → negative (−f)

### 5.5 Lens Formula and Magnification
- **Lens Formula**: `1/v − 1/u = 1/f`
- **Magnification**: `m = v/u = height of image (hᵢ) / height of object (hₒ)`
  - m > 0 → virtual, erect image
  - m < 0 → real, inverted image
  - |m| > 1 → magnified; |m| < 1 → diminished

### 5.6 Power of a Lens
- **Power**: ability of a lens to converge or diverge light  
  `P = 1/f` (f in metres)
- SI unit: **Dioptre (D)**
- Convex lens: P > 0 (positive)
- Concave lens: P < 0 (negative)
- Combination of lenses in contact: `P = P₁ + P₂ + P₃ + …`

### 5.7 Uses of Lenses
- Convex lens: camera, projector, magnifying glass, spectacles for hypermetropia, microscope, telescope
- Concave lens: spectacles for myopia, viewfinder of cameras, telescopes (Galilean)

### 5.8 Defects of Vision and Correction

| Defect | Cause | Symptom | Correction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Myopia (Short-sightedness) | Eyeball too long / lens too convex | Cannot see distant objects clearly | Concave lens (diverging) |
| Hypermetropia (Long-sightedness) | Eyeball too short / lens too flat | Cannot see near objects clearly | Convex lens (converging) |
| Presbyopia | Loss of elasticity of eye lens (old age) | Both near and far vision affected | Bifocal lens |

## Key Formulae

| Quantity | Formula | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| Lens Formula | `1/v − 1/u = 1/f` | — |
| Magnification | `m = v/u` | dimensionless |
| Power | `P = 1/f(m)` | Dioptre (D) |
| Combined power | `P = P₁ + P₂` | Dioptre (D) |

---

## Important Terms

- **Focal Length**: Distance from optical centre to principal focus
- **Power of a Lens**: Reciprocal of focal length in metres
- **Magnification**: Ratio of image size to object size
- **Myopia**: Defect of vision corrected by concave lens
- **Hypermetropia**: Defect of vision corrected by convex lens
