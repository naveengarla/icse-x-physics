## Topics Covered

### 7.1 Nature of Sound
- Sound is a **mechanical, longitudinal wave** — needs a material medium to travel
- Cannot travel through vacuum
- Speed in air at 0°C ≈ 332 m/s; increases with temperature (~0.6 m/s per °C rise)
- Speed: solids > liquids > gases

### 7.2 Reflection of Sound Waves
- Sound obeys the same laws of reflection as light:
  1. Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection
  2. Incident ray, reflected ray and normal are in the same plane
- **Echo**: repetition of sound due to reflection from a distant hard surface
  - Minimum distance for echo: `d = v × t/2` ≈ **17 m** (at 340 m/s, persistence = 0.1 s)
  - Human ear can distinguish two sounds if they arrive at least 0.1 s apart
- **Applications of echoes**:
  - **SONAR**: detects underwater objects; `depth = v × t/2` (using ultrasound)
  - **Ultrasound scanning** (sonography) in medicine
  - Detecting flaws/cracks in metals
  - Measuring depth of sea (echo sounding)
  - Bats and dolphins use echolocation for navigation

### 7.3 Reverberation
- **Reverberation**: persistence of sound in an enclosed space due to repeated reflections
- Time gap between original and reflected sound < 0.1 s → not distinguished as echo
- Undesirable in concert halls / auditoriums — deadens sound quality
- **Reducing reverberation**:
  - Use sound-absorbing materials on walls/ceiling (curtains, carpets, acoustic tiles)
  - Curved or slanted surfaces to prevent focusing of sound

### 7.4 Natural, Damped and Forced Vibrations

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Free / Natural** | Body vibrates at its natural frequency; amplitude stays constant (ideal) | Tuning fork struck once in vacuum |
| **Damped** | Amplitude decreases due to energy loss (friction/air resistance) | Pendulum in air |
| **Forced** | Body vibrates at frequency of external periodic force, not its natural frequency | A table vibrating due to a motor on it |

### 7.5 Resonance
- **Resonance**: special case of forced vibrations where the frequency of the applied force equals the **natural frequency** of the body → maximum amplitude
- **Conditions**: external frequency = natural frequency of body
- **Examples of resonance**:
  - Tuning forks of same frequency vibrating together
  - Tuning a radio receiver to a broadcast frequency
  - Resonance column to determine speed of sound in air
  - Shattering of glass by a loud note at its natural frequency
  - Tacoma Narrows Bridge collapse (1940)
  - Soldiers breaking step on bridges (to avoid resonance)

### 7.6 Characteristics of Sound

| Characteristic | Physical Basis | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Loudness** | Amplitude of vibration | Measured in decibels (dB); greater amplitude → louder |
| **Pitch** | Frequency of vibration | Higher frequency → shriller / higher pitch |
| **Quality (Timbre)** | Waveform / presence of overtones | Distinguishes same note from different instruments |

- **Intensity**: power per unit area; SI unit W/m²
  - Threshold of hearing: 10⁻¹² W/m²
  - Loudness level (dB): `L = 10 log₁₀(I/I₀)`

### 7.7 Ultrasound and Infrasound
| Type | Frequency Range | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Infrasound | < 20 Hz | Earthquakes, some animal communication (elephants) |
| Audible sound | 20 Hz – 20,000 Hz | Human hearing |
| Ultrasound | > 20,000 Hz | Bats, dolphins, SONAR, medical imaging, industrial cleaning |

- **Applications of ultrasound**:
  - Medical: diagnosis (sonography), breaking kidney stones (lithotripsy)
  - Industrial: cleaning delicate instruments, detecting cracks
  - Navigation: SONAR, echolocation

### 7.8 Noise Pollution
- Sources: traffic, industry, loudspeakers, aircraft
- Effects: hearing damage, stress, sleep disturbance, high blood pressure
- Control: soundproofing, green belts, noise barriers, regulations

## Important Terms

- **Echo**: A distinct repetition of sound heard after reflection from a hard surface
- **Reverberation**: Prolongation of sound in an enclosed space due to multiple reflections
- **Resonance**: Maximum amplitude vibration when driving frequency = natural frequency
- **Pitch**: Sensation corresponding to frequency of sound
- **Ultrasound**: Sound with frequency above 20,000 Hz
