## 1. Board Core - Must Know

### Moment of Force

**Definition:** The moment of a force about a point is the product of the force and the perpendicular distance of the line of action of the force from that point.

$$\text{Moment of force} = F \times d$$

Where:
- $F$ = force in newton (N)
- $d$ = perpendicular distance in metre (m)
- SI unit = N m

**Do not write joule as the unit of moment.** Work and moment have the same dimensions, but they are different physical quantities.

### Couple

**Definition:** A couple is a pair of equal and opposite parallel forces acting along different lines of action.

$$\text{Moment of a couple} = F \times d$$

Where $d$ is the perpendicular distance between the two forces.

Key point: A couple has zero resultant force but non-zero moment, so it produces rotation.

### Principle of Moments

**Statement:** When a body is in rotational equilibrium, the sum of clockwise moments about a point is equal to the sum of anticlockwise moments about the same point.

$$\sum M_{CW} = \sum M_{ACW}$$

### Equilibrium

A body is in equilibrium if it does not change its state of rest or uniform motion in a straight line.

Conditions:
- Resultant force = 0
- Resultant moment = 0

### Centre of Gravity

**Definition:** The centre of gravity of a body is the point through which the resultant weight of the body acts, irrespective of the orientation of the body.

For a uniform regular body, the centre of gravity lies at its geometrical centre.

### Uniform Circular Motion

In uniform circular motion:
- Speed is constant.
- Velocity changes because direction changes.
- Acceleration is directed toward the centre.
- Centripetal force is directed toward the centre.

$$a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}$$

$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Centrifugal force is apparent or pseudo force observed in a rotating frame. It is not a real interaction force in the inertial frame.

## 3. Solved Examples

### Example 1 - Moment of Force

**Question:** A force of 20 N acts at a perpendicular distance of 0.5 m from a pivot. Find the moment of force.

**Solution:**

$$M = F \times d$$

$$M = 20 \times 0.5 = 10 \text{ N m}$$

**Answer:** Moment of force = 10 N m.

### Example 2 - Force at the Pivot

**Question:** A force of 500 N acts through the pivot of a lever. Find its moment about the pivot.

**Reasoning:** The line of action passes through the pivot, so perpendicular distance is zero.

$$M = F \times d = 500 \times 0 = 0$$

**Answer:** Moment = 0 N m.

### Example 3 - Principle of Moments

**Question:** A metre rule is pivoted at the 50 cm mark. A 100 gf weight is placed at the 20 cm mark. What weight must be placed at the 80 cm mark to balance the rule?

**Solution:**

Distance of 100 gf from pivot:

$$50 - 20 = 30 \text{ cm}$$

Distance of unknown weight from pivot:

$$80 - 50 = 30 \text{ cm}$$

Using principle of moments:

$$100 \times 30 = W \times 30$$

$$W = 100 \text{ gf}$$

**Answer:** Required weight = 100 gf.

### Example 4 - Include Weight of the Rule

**Question:** A uniform metre rule of weight 80 gf is pivoted at the 40 cm mark. A 120 gf weight is hung at the 20 cm mark. What weight must be hung at the 80 cm mark for equilibrium?

**Reasoning:** Since the rule is uniform, its own weight acts at the 50 cm mark.

Distances from pivot:
- 120 gf at 20 cm: $40 - 20 = 20$ cm, anticlockwise
- Rule weight 80 gf at 50 cm: $50 - 40 = 10$ cm, clockwise
- Unknown W at 80 cm: $80 - 40 = 40$ cm, clockwise

Using principle of moments:

$$120 \times 20 = 80 \times 10 + W \times 40$$

$$2400 = 800 + 40W$$

$$40W = 1600$$

$$W = 40 \text{ gf}$$

**Answer:** Required weight = 40 gf.

### Example 5 - Couple

**Question:** Two equal and opposite forces of 15 N act as a couple. The perpendicular distance between them is 0.4 m. Find the moment of the couple.

$$M = F \times d = 15 \times 0.4 = 6 \text{ N m}$$

**Answer:** Moment of couple = 6 N m.

### Example 6 - Centripetal Force

**Question:** A 0.5 kg stone is whirled in a circle of radius 1 m at a speed of 4 m/s. Find the centripetal force.

$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

$$F_c = \frac{0.5 \times 4^2}{1} = 8 \text{ N}$$

**Answer:** Centripetal force = 8 N, directed toward the centre.

## 4. Board-Style Short Answers

### Q1. Why is the handle of a door fixed far from the hinge?

The handle is fixed far from the hinge to increase the perpendicular distance from the pivot. Since moment of force = force x perpendicular distance, a larger distance gives a larger turning effect for the same force.

### Q2. Why is the unit of moment not joule?

Moment of force and work both have dimensions of N m, but they represent different physical quantities. Work involves displacement in the direction of force, while moment involves perpendicular distance from a pivot. Therefore the unit of moment is written as N m, not joule.

### Q3. Why does a body moving in a circle at constant speed have acceleration?

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity. In circular motion, the speed remains constant but the direction of velocity changes continuously. Therefore the velocity changes and the body has centripetal acceleration.

### Q4. Why is centrifugal force called a pseudo force?

Centrifugal force is observed only in a rotating, non-inertial frame. In an inertial frame, there is no real outward force acting on the body. The apparent outward effect is due to inertia, so centrifugal force is called a pseudo force.

### Q5. Why does an object topple when tilted too far?

An object topples when the vertical line through its centre of gravity falls outside its base of support. Then the weight produces a moment that turns the object over.

## 5. Common Exam Mistakes

| Mistake | Correct Method |
|:---|:---|
| Using distance from hand to pivot for moment | Use perpendicular distance from pivot to line of action |
| Writing joule as unit of moment | Write N m |
| Forgetting ruler's own weight | Include it unless stated negligible or pivoted at its CG |
| Mixing gf cm and N m | Use one unit system throughout |
| Saying centrifugal force is a real action-reaction force | It is pseudo/apparent; not a third-law pair with centripetal force |
| Saying constant speed means no acceleration in a circle | Direction changes, so velocity changes |
| Saying all rotations need a fixed pivot | Free bodies can rotate due to off-centre force or couple |

## 7. Answer Key

### A. One-Mark Answers

1. Moment of force is force multiplied by the perpendicular distance of its line of action from the pivot.
2. N m.
3. A couple is two equal and opposite parallel forces acting along different lines of action.
4. In equilibrium, sum of clockwise moments equals sum of anticlockwise moments about the same point.
5. At its geometrical centre.
6. Toward the centre of the circular path.

### B. Two-Mark Answers

1. Near the handle, the perpendicular distance from hinge is larger. Hence the moment is larger for the same force.
2. Centripetal force is real and acts toward the centre. Centrifugal force is apparent/pseudo and appears outward in a rotating frame.
3. When the string breaks, centripetal force is removed. By inertia, the stone continues in its instantaneous direction of motion, which is tangent to the circle.
4. A wide base makes it harder for the vertical line through the CG to fall outside the base, so the body is more stable.

### C. Numerical Answers

1. $d = 25$ cm = 0.25 m. Moment = $30 \times 0.25 = 7.5$ N m.
2. Moment of couple = $40 \times 0.2 = 8$ N m.
3. Distances: 60 gf is 20 cm from pivot; W is 30 cm from pivot. $60 \times 20 = W \times 30$, so $W = 40$ gf.
4. $F_c = mv^2/r = 1000 \times 10^2 / 50 = 2000$ N.
5. Distances from 30 cm pivot: 200 gf at 10 cm is 20 cm left; rule weight 100 gf at 50 cm is 20 cm right; W at 70 cm is 40 cm right.  
   $200 \times 20 = 100 \times 20 + W \times 40$  
   $4000 = 2000 + 40W$  
   $W = 50$ gf.
