## Topics Covered

### 4.1 Refraction of Light
- **Refraction**: bending of light as it passes from one transparent medium to another due to change in speed
- Light travels fastest in vacuum (`c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s`) and slows down in denser media

#### Laws of Refraction
1. The incident ray, the refracted ray and the normal at the point of incidence — all lie in the same plane
2. **Snell's Law**: `n₁ sin i = n₂ sin r`  
   (ratio of sin of angle of incidence to sin of angle of refraction is constant for a given pair of media)

### 4.2 Refractive Index
- **Absolute refractive index** of a medium:  
  `n = speed of light in vacuum / speed of light in medium = c/v`
- **Relative refractive index**: `₁n₂ = n₂/n₁ = sin i / sin r`
- Denser medium → higher refractive index → light bends towards normal
- Rarer medium → lower refractive index → light bends away from normal

| Medium | Approx. Refractive Index |
|---|---|
| Vacuum / Air | 1.00 |
| Water | 1.33 |
| Glass (crown) | 1.52 |
| Diamond | 2.42 |

### 4.3 Refraction Through a Rectangular Glass Slab
- Incident ray and emergent ray are **parallel** (no angular deviation) but **laterally displaced**
- **Lateral displacement**: perpendicular distance between incident and emergent rays
- Lateral displacement increases with: greater angle of incidence, greater thickness, greater refractive index

### 4.4 Total Internal Reflection (TIR)
- Occurs when light travels from a **denser** to a **rarer** medium
- **Critical Angle (C)**: angle of incidence in the denser medium for which angle of refraction = 90°
  - `sin C = n_rarer / n_denser = 1/n` (when rarer medium is air)
- When angle of incidence **exceeds** the critical angle → **Total Internal Reflection**

#### Conditions for TIR:
1. Light must travel from denser to rarer medium
2. Angle of incidence must be greater than the critical angle

#### Applications of TIR:

| Application | How TIR is used |
|---|---|
| **Optical Fibres** | Light undergoes repeated TIR along the fibre; used in telecommunications and medical endoscopy |
| **Reflecting Prisms** | 45°-45°-90° prism used in periscopes, binoculars to turn light by 90° or 180° |
| **Mirage** | Hot air near ground is less dense; light from sky undergoes TIR → appears as water on road |
| **Sparkling of Diamond** | Very small critical angle (24°); light undergoes multiple TIR inside |
| **Looming** | Cold air near sea surface; objects appear elevated |

## Important Terms

- **Refraction**: Change in direction (and speed) of light at an interface between two media
- **Refractive Index**: Ratio of speed of light in vacuum to speed in the medium
- **Critical Angle**: The angle of incidence beyond which TIR occurs
- **Total Internal Reflection**: Complete reflection of light back into denser medium
- **Optical Fibre**: Thin transparent fibre that transmits light via repeated TIR
