## Topics Covered

### 9.1 The Three Wires in a Household Circuit
| Wire | Colour (India) | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Live (L)** | Red / Brown | Carries current at high potential (230 V) |
| **Neutral (N)** | Black / Blue | Completes circuit; at zero potential |
| **Earth (E)** | Green / Yellow-Green | Safety wire; connected to metal casing of appliance |

- AC supply in India: 230 V, 50 Hz

### 9.2 Wiring System in a House
- **Main switch**: disconnects entire supply
- **Meter**: measures electricity consumed in kWh
- **Distribution board (fuse box)**: distributes to different circuits via fuses/MCBs
- Two main circuits:
  - **Lighting circuit**: lower current (5 A fuse)
  - **Power circuit**: higher current (15 A fuse) for heavy appliances
- All appliances connected in **parallel** → same 230 V across each; can be switched independently

### 9.3 Earthing (Grounding)
- Metal casing of appliances is connected to earth through green wire
- If live wire accidentally touches casing → current flows to earth → fuse blows → circuit breaks
- **Purpose**: prevents electric shock to user; provides safe path for fault current
- Earthing is compulsory for metallic appliances (washing machines, geysers, refrigerators)

### 9.4 Safety Devices

#### Fuse
- A thin wire of low melting point alloy (tin-lead / tinned copper) that **melts** when excessive current flows
- Connected in **series** with the live wire
- **Rating**: maximum safe current it allows (1 A, 2 A, 5 A, 15 A, etc.)
- Choosing fuse: `I_fuse rating > normal operating current` but as close as possible
- Rewirable fuses vs cartridge fuses

#### MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker)
- Automatically trips (disconnects) when current exceeds rated value
- Advantage over fuse: can be reset without replacement
- Uses electromagnetic or bimetallic strip mechanism

#### ELCB / RCD (Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker / Residual Current Device)
- Detects even small leakage current to earth (as low as 30 mA)
- Trips in milliseconds → prevents electrocution
- Essential in bathrooms, kitchens, outdoor sockets

### 9.5 Short Circuit
- **Cause**: live and neutral wires come into direct contact (damaged insulation)
- **Effect**: very high current flows (resistance ≈ 0) → overheating → fire risk
- **Prevention**: fuses and MCBs, proper insulation

### 9.6 Overloading
- **Cause**: connecting too many high-power appliances to the same circuit
- **Effect**: wire overheats → insulation melts → fire
- **Prevention**: not exceeding rated capacity; using separate circuits for heavy appliances

### 9.7 Electrical Safety Precautions
- Never touch a switch or appliance with wet hands
- Use rubber-soled footwear
- Switch off before replacing fuses or doing repairs
- Do not use damaged wires or plugs
- Do not overload sockets with adaptors
- Keep electrical appliances away from water

### 9.8 Power Consumed by Appliances
- Each appliance is rated in watts: e.g., bulb 60 W, geyser 2000 W
- Units consumed = `Power (kW) × Time (hours)`
- Fuse rating ≥ `P/V = I`

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Key Point |
|---|---|
| Fuse | Melts to break circuit; in series with live wire |
| Earthing | Connects metal casing to earth for safety |
| Short circuit | Accidental live-neutral contact → huge current |
| Overloading | Too many appliances → excess current → heat |
| MCB | Reusable switch-type safety device |

---

## Important Terms

- **Fuse**: Safety device that melts and breaks circuit when current is too high
- **Earthing**: Connecting metal body of appliance to the earth for safety
- **Short Circuit**: Fault where live and neutral wires connect, causing very high current
- **MCB**: Miniature Circuit Breaker — reusable alternative to a fuse
- **ELCB**: Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker — protects against small earth fault currents
